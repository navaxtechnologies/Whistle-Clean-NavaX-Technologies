# Module 2 — Competitive Landscape & Pricing Intelligence

Live web research, June 2026. Revenue estimates use a review-velocity method (lifetime reviews ÷ months ÷ ~25% capture rate × avg ticket) and are labeled ESTIMATE.

## Part A — Top 5 San Antonio competitors (interior cleaning)

### 1. Sparkly Maid San Antonio — review-velocity leader
- **Reviews:** **929 @ 5.0★** (Birdeye). Site: sparklymaidsanantonio.com — modern, programmatic neighborhood landing pages, flat-rate-by-size, instant online booking.
- **Model:** flat-rate by home size; recurring plans; deep, post-construction, eco; 24-hr re-clean guarantee; Latina-owned.
- **Est. revenue:** ~38 reviews/mo → ~150 jobs/mo × ~$170 ≈ **$25K/mo** (ESTIMATE).
- **Better than us:** huge review moat, programmatic local SEO, instant booking. **We're better:** published transparent prices (they hide behind a quote), explicit property-manager/turnover focus, lower entry price.

### 2. Maids on a Mission — trust/brand leader
- **Reviews:** **847 @ 4.9★**; "600+ five-star Google." ~10+ yrs, W-2 employees, veteran-/woman-owned, #1 SA "Cleaning for a Reason" partner (free cleans for cancer patients — strong PR).
- **Offer:** $25 off code "SAVENOW"; satisfaction guarantee. Quote-based, no public prices.
- **Est. revenue:** ~24 reviews/mo → **$20–30K/mo** (ESTIMATE).
- **Better than us:** decade of trust, W-2 consistency, charity hook, review base. **We're better:** transparent flat pricing; lower-friction entry products; leaner overhead.

### 3. White Glove Maid Service — longevity leader
- **Since 1983** (40+ yrs, "250,000+ cleanings"); A+ BBB. **Phone-first booking** (a weakness). Offers laundry & dry carpet cleaning.
- **Est. revenue:** **$40–70K/mo** if still at scale (ESTIMATE).
- **Better than us:** unmatched credibility, 40-yr referral base. **We're better:** modern transparent pricing, online booking (they're phone-only), faster/cheaper entry.

### 4. MaidPro San Antonio — systemized franchise
- **Reviews:** **266 @ 4.7★** (lower than locals). 49-Point Checklist™. Data point: $96 for a 3-hr biweekly on 1,800 sq ft (~$32/hr). Online estimate + franchise CRM.
- **Est. revenue:** **$30–50K/mo** (franchise AUV norm, ESTIMATE).
- **Better than us:** systems, standardization, brand, booking tech. **We're better:** local ownership/personal touch, transparent flat pricing, lower price, PM/turnover specialization.

### 5. Vanguard Cleaning Systems (+ Anago, Corvus, System4) — commercial/office
- B2B janitorial franchises, custom per-sq-ft contracts, day-porter capacity. Reputation via contracts, not Google reviews.
- **Est. revenue:** **$50K+/mo** at network level (ESTIMATE).
- **Better than us:** established B2B contracts, scale. **We're better:** we can win the **small offices, laundry rooms, and apartment-turn work that are too small for Vanguard/Anago to prioritize** — that underserved niche is our wedge.

**Takeaway:** every strong residential competitor **hides pricing behind a quote** — our transparent flat pricing is a real differentiator. The moat we lack is **review volume** (they have 800–900; our 48 are siloed on Thumbtack). And the **open lane** is small-commercial + apartment-complex turnover/laundry work.

## Part B — National benchmark: **Molly Maid** ($100K+/mo model)

The clearest documented blueprint for a recurring-heavy interior operator at scale.

- **Revenue model:** **~90% of 1.7M annual cleanings are repeat customers** — overwhelmingly recurring (weekly/biweekly/monthly). Recurring is the entire engine of stability.
- **Unit economics:** AUV **$759K–$855K/yr (~$63–71K/mo)**; top units >$100K/mo; ~16% owner net.
- **Tech stack:** **CLEO** scheduling/dispatch; CRM with capacity planning + automated reminders; standardized checklists/SOPs.
- **Team model:** **executive ownership** — owner runs teams, doesn't clean; W-2 two-person crews; territories of 45K–70K households.
- **Top 3 channels:** local SEO + branded booking site; recurring-customer retention & referrals (the 90% repeat base); paid local advertising.

**What Whistle Clean copies directly:**
1. **Make recurring the core offer** (we're 100% one-off today). 90% recurring is provably achievable.
2. **Adopt a real booking/CRM tool** — not CLEO; the affordable equivalents: **ZenMaid (~$49/mo)** or **Launch27 (~$75/mo)** `[COST: $49–75/mo]` `[NAVAX]` — online booking, recurring billing, auto-reminders, crew dispatch.
3. **Publish a standardized checklist** (Molly's consistency edge / MaidPro's 49-point list) so non-owner cleaners deliver uniform quality.
4. **Build the recurring-billing + auto-reminder retention loop** — highest-leverage single move.

## Part C — San Antonio pricing intelligence → **you are underpriced 20–40%**

| Service | SA market rate |
|---|---|
| Standard clean, ~500 sq ft (apt) | **$143** |
| Standard clean, 1,500 sq ft | $182 |
| Deep clean, ~500 sq ft | **$287** |

*(Sources: Lupe's House Cleaning SA, HomeYou, HomeAdvisor, ISSA, Janitorial Leads Pro — links in research notes.)*

**Verdict: ~20–40% below market on standard cleans; the +$40 deep-clean add-on is 60–85% below the $100–$260 premium the market commands; move-outs are likely billed too low.**

### Recommended repricing (to lower-market — still undercuts everyone) `[JOSE/FABRIZIO ACTION]`
- Efficiency $95 → **$120–$130** · 1×1 $115 → **$145** · 2×2 $125 → **$165** · 3×2 $135 → **$185**
- Heavy/deep add-on $40 → **$80–$120** (≈ doubles margin on the hardest job)
- Touch-up: keep $60 as a foot-in-the-door **but require a 2-visit minimum or convert to recurring** (a one-off $60 visit barely clears drive time + labor at SA's $35/cleaner-hr floor)
- **Add a separate move-out tier** (market is +30–70% over standard for the same size) — currently the biggest silent leak
- **Add a recurring discount ladder** (weekly 15% / biweekly 10% / monthly 5% off) to build the Molly-Maid recurring engine while protecting per-job price

> These increases raise revenue per job ~25–40% with **zero new leads** — the fastest dollar in the whole plan. Full economics in Module 7.
