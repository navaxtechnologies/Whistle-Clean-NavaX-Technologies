## 1.2 — Revenue-leak methodology

Assumes a conservative **$130 average interior job** (below SA market — see Module 2) and that captured demand converts at normal local-service rates.

- **GBP unclaimed/unoptimized → $1,000+/mo.** The Google map pack is the highest-intent local channel ("house cleaning near me"). A claimed, photo-rich, review-fed profile typically drives 8–15 leads/mo for a local cleaner; missing it forfeits ~6–8 jobs/mo × $130 ≈ $800–$1,000+.
- **Reviews siloed on Thumbtack → ranking + trust loss.** 48 Thumbtack reviews not mirrored to Google means Google sees a thin profile; competitors with 800+ Google reviews (Module 2) outrank you. Migrating even a fraction lifts map-pack rank.
- **Fragmented identity → $400/mo.** Five name variants and conflicting phones split SEO authority and confuse callers; some simply give up or call a competitor.
- **No TikTok/YouTube → $300–$450/mo.** Cleaning transformations (deep cleans, move-out before/afters) are among the most viral home-service content; zero presence = zero organic top-of-funnel.

**Total estimated digital-footprint leak: ~$3,000–$4,500/mo** of demand the reputation already earns but doesn't capture (full leak across all bottlenecks, incl. model gaps, is in Module 3).
