# Module 4 — San Antonio Market Opportunity

Live research, June 2026. Hard numbers carry sources; derived figures labeled ESTIMATE.

## Part A — Market size & demand

- **Metro:** ~1.24M city population; ~$121B economy; Texas' 3rd-largest metro. *(Dallas Fed)*
- **Homeownership: 52.2%** (2024) — far below the 65.2% national rate. **→ ~48% of San Antonio households RENT.** This is the single most important fact in this report: the renter base is the engine of **move-out / turnover cleaning** demand. *(Census / CultureMap)*
- **Median home value:** ~$235,700. **Median build year ~1984** → ~half the housing stock is 40+ years old (older homes = more deep-clean and move-out resale-prep demand). *(Data USA, Point2Homes)*
- **Housing units (city):** ~524K baseline (dated); true 2026 count materially higher. ESTIMATE 600K–650K. *(Census)*
- **Seasonality (residential):** peaks **spring (Apr–May)** and **Nov–Dec (holiday)**; off-season can idle 30–40% of capacity.

## Part B — The real goldmine: renters, turnover & recurring commercial

**Apartments / multifamily:**
- **14,515 new multifamily units delivered in 2024** (+31% YoY); ~8,000 more in 2025. Each new unit needs first-occupancy/post-construction cleaning. *(MMG, Matthews)*
- **San Antonio has the HIGHEST apartment vacancy of the top-50 US markets — ~15.7% (Q1 2026).** High vacancy + record supply = **constant unit turnover = sustained make-ready/turn-clean demand.** *(CultureMap, Yardi)*
- ESTIMATE: with ~48% renters and high churn, the metro generates **well over 100,000 lease turns/year** — each a potential turn-clean.

**Move-out / turnover seasonality:** peaks **May–September** (school-calendar lease cycles; rents peak Jun–Jul) **plus an end-of-every-month spike.** Complementary to residential's spring/holiday peaks — a blended book keeps crews busy year-round. Add **military PCS cycles** (Randolph/Lackland/Fort Sam) for steady move-in/out volume.
